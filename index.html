Gallery of Games

Super Mario Brothers

A platform game developed and published by Nintendo. The successor to the 1983 arcade game Mario Bros. and the first in the Super Mario series, it was released in 1985 for the Famicom in Japan. Following a limited US release for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), it was ported to international arcades for the Nintendo Vs. System in early 1986. The NES version received a wide release in North America that year and in PAL regions in 1987.

Sonic The Hedgehog

Sonic the Hedgehog is a platform video game developed by Sonic Team and published by Sega for the Sega Genesis home video game console. The first game in the Sonic the Hedgehog franchise, it was released in North America in June 1991 and in PAL regions and Japan the following month. The game features Sonic the Hedgehog, who can run at supersonic speeds, setting out on a quest to defeat Dr. Robotnik, a scientist who has imprisoned animals in robots and seeks the powerful Chaos Emeralds. Sonic was originally created to overthrow Nintendo's monopoly on the video game market in the early 1990's. The plan worked and forced compitition between the two companies, which allowed some amazing games to be created. Once true 3D games started being created (1996), Sega abandoned the 2d sonic formula for twelve years in their mainline games. These 3d games are loved by many but serve as a point of criticism as to whether sonic is better in 2d or 3d.

Pokemon

Pokemon is a series of video games developed by Game Freak and published by Nintendo and The Pokemon Company under the Pokemon media franchise. It was created by Satoshi Tajiri with assistance from Ken Sugimori, the first games, Pocket Monsters Red and Green, released in 1996 in Japan for the game Boy, later released outside of Japan as Pokemon Red and Blue.

It has been publised byseveral companies for multiple platforms, most prominently during a dispute over the appropriation of the rights inthe late 1980s. After a significant period of publication by Nintento, the right reverted to Pajitnov in 1996, who co-founded the Tetris Company with Henk Rogers to manage licensing.

Kirby

Kirby is a platform game developed by HAL Laboratories and released in 1992. Mainly published by Nintendo, Kirby has become a household name seen alongside Mario. The simplistic, but cute design of Kirby has attracted many new, young fans in it's 30 years of history.

Doom
