Gallery of Games

Super Mario Brothers

A platform game developed and published by Nintendo. The successor to the 1983 arcade game Mario Bros. and the first in the Super Mario series, it was released in 1985 for the Famicom in Japan. Following a limited US release for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), it was ported to international arcades for the Nintendo Vs. System in early 1986. The NES version received a wide release in North America that year and in PAL regions in 1987.

Sonic The Hedgehog

Sonic the Hedgehog is a platform video game developed by Sonic Team and published by Sega for the Sega Genesis home video game console. The first game in the Sonic the Hedgehog franchise, it was released in North America in June 1991 and in PAL regions and Japan the following month. The game features Sonic the Hedgehog, who can run at supersonic speeds, setting out on a quest to defeat Dr. Robotnik, a scientist who has imprisoned animals in robots and seeks the powerful Chaos Emeralds. Sonic was originally created to overthrow Nintendo's monopoly on the video game market in the early 1990's. The plan worked and forced compitition between the two companies, which allowed some amazing games to be created. Once true 3D games started being created (1996), Sega abandoned the 2d sonic formula for twelve years in their mainline games. These 3d games are loved by many but serve as a point of criticism as to whether sonic is better in 2d or 3d.

Hollow Knight

Hollow Knight is a 2017 Metroidvania action-adventure game devloped and published by independent developer Team Cherry. In the game, the Player controls the Knight, a nameless insectoid warrior who explores Hallownest, a fallen kingdom plagued by a supernatural disease. The game is set in diverse subterranean locations, and it features friendly and hostile bug-like characters and numerous bosses. Players have the opportunity to unlock new abilities as they explore each location, along with pieces of lore and flavor text that are spread throughout the kingdom.

Journey

Journey is an indie adventure game co-developed by Thatgamecompany and Santa Monica Studio, published by Sony Computer Entertainment, and directed by Jenova Chen. It was initially released for the PS3 in March 2012, and ported to the PS4 in July of 2015. The player controls a robed figure in a vast desert, traveling towards a mountain in the distance. Other players on the same journey can be discovered, and two players can meet and assist each other, but they can only communicate via musical chimes, and cannot see each other's names until after the game's credits. These chimes transform dull pieces of cloth found throughout the levels into vibrant red, affecting the game world and allowing the player to progress through the levels.

Legend of Zelda

The Legend of Zelda was first released in 1986 by Nintendo and has since become a household name with 26 entries in the main series alone. The game follows our hero, Link, as he slashes and ‘hya’s his way to defeat an ultimate evil. From humble beginnings saving princesses, the game has expanded to fighting ancient deities and defeating the embodiments of true evil. But no matter what, The Legend of Zelda franchise stays true to its dungeon crawler elements, incorporating the puzzles and exploration that has inspired fans and game designers for decades.

Dark Souls

The Dark Souls franchise, developed by FromSoftware, has left its mark on gaming history. Leading the charge in the genre of brutal and difficult games, the audience it has built has no problem torturing themselves, dying, and restarting again and again. In the dark fantasy world of Dark Souls a slight object out of place might be an indicator of your certain death, but it is up to you to brave the darkness and keep the fire burning. Dark Souls’ fame has spawned many other similar titles from different developers, coining the term ‘Soulslike’.

Terraria

Terraria was released in 2011 by the small indie team Re-Logic. The games initial years were turbulent, with constant accusations of being a 2D Minecraft rip-off. The game’s turbulent development further drove away some of its audience. However, those who stuck with the game witnessed the game grow far past the criticisms. Terraria has since grown a notable cult following, expanding its gameplay and player base with each content update. Other 2D sandbox games have drawn inspiration from Terraria in its progression and overworld design.
